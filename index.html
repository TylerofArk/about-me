Bio

I was raised outside of city limits, or as we called it “the country.” I grew up trying to catch crawdads in the creek, climbing trees and sitting around more campfires than I can count. I joined the Marine Corps in 2009 as a combat correspondent and undertook 8 years of learning the trade of photography and videography while telling the story of our nations service members. The constant of those chapters of life is the outdoors. Although my military work taught me to focus on capturing action that tells a story, it was the beauty of the environments that really spoke to me. My childhood memories of growing up outside are my inspiration. Memories of being raised on 10 acres of land in the Ozarks, where we fished in the summer for some of our meals and spent nearly every day in the woods. Memories of riding up and down hilly red dirt roads on my bike, exploring what I thought was the whole world — memories of the summers on Beaver Lake, jumping off rocks and swimming in the cold clear water next to the dam. All my favorite memories of life have been outside. It’s with that passion that I take on every project and share the magic of the great outdoors and the fire it lights in the soul.
